About Me

Curiosity, forged in Mumbai.

I'm a fifth-year Physics PhD candidate at Purdue University, working in Dr. Jonathan Hood's laboratory. I grew up in Mumbai, where curiosity became a core part of who I am — and that hasn't changed.

My doctoral research sits at the intersection of atomic, molecular, and optical (AMO) physics and quantum engineering. I build and operate optical-tweezer platforms that trap and cool individual lithium and cesium atoms down to the microkelvin regime.

Beyond the lab, I write — both physics blogs that explore ideas I find fascinating, and poetry that emerges from the quieter corners of a scientific life.

Research

Engineering Quantum Matter,
One Atom at a Time.

Optical-tweezer experiments that cool, trap, and assemble atoms into exotic dipolar molecules — building blocks for fault-tolerant quantum processors.

01 · LITHIUM

First ⁶Li Tweezer Trapping & Record Imaging

Upon joining the lab in 2021, I helped commission a dual-species chamber and engineer a 671 nm Zeeman slower to capture the first single ⁶Li atom in a 1064 nm optical tweezer. We achieved 2,000 consecutive images with 99.95% survival probability — setting the benchmark for neutral-atom imaging fidelity and supplying the lithium component for LiCs molecule assembly.

671 nm Zeeman Slower 99.95% Survival
02 · THEORY

Unified Sub-Doppler Cooling Framework

The efficiency seen with lithium exposed gaps in the literature. We developed a master-equation model treating motional and hyperfine dynamics on equal footing, integrating polarization-gradient, gray-molasses, Λ-enhanced, and Raman/SSB schemes into a single analytic formalism — producing a cooling phase diagram now widely used by AMO groups for ground-state preparation.

Master Equation Gray Molasses
03 · CESIUM

Narrow-Line Quadrupole Cooling of ¹³³Cs

We exploited the narrow 6S₁∕₂ → 5D₅∕₂ electric-quadrupole transition (λ = 685 nm, Γ/2π ≈ 117 kHz) using a Laguerre–Gaussian vortex beam for background-free imaging. This delivered 99.58% state-detection fidelity and cooled Cs to 5 µK (⟨n_rad⟩ ≈ 0.8) — the first quadrupole narrow-line cooling of an alkali atom in a tweezer.

685 nm Quadrupole LG Vortex Beam
04 · OUTLOOK

Toward Dipolar LiCs Quantum Processors

Li control, the unified cooling theory, and the Cs quadrupole toolbox jointly enable deterministic assembly of ground-state LiCs molecules with a permanent dipole moment of ~5.5 Debye — the largest among bi-alkali dimers — supporting fast, high-fidelity two-qubit gates in scalable neutral-atom quantum-information architectures.

Teaching

Sharing the Joy of Physics.

Teaching is integral to my identity as a scientist. I've served as a teaching assistant and instructor at Purdue, working to make physics accessible and exciting.

Modern Mechanics & Modern Physics Labs
Graduate Teaching Assistant · Purdue University
Ran weekly lab sessions, wrote grading rubrics, and guided over a hundred undergraduates through optics and mechanics experiments. Integrated rigorous data-analysis training during office hours, building research-level analytical skills.
AMO Physics for M.Sc. Students
Mini-Course Instructor · University of Mumbai · Summer 2023
Invited to deliver an eight-lecture series on atomic, molecular, and optical physics. Combined laser-cooling simulations with structured assignments, introducing current optical-tweezer techniques to M.Sc. students in India.
Quantum Explorer App
Creator & Developer · quantumexplorer.streamlit.app
Built an interactive web app to make quantum concepts visual and intuitive — Bloch sphere, quantum gates, superposition, entanglement, and Born-rule measurement. No physics background required. Publicly available and freely accessible.
Online AMO Lecture Series
Creator · YouTube Channel
Developed publicly available online lectures for AMO beginners: three foundational lectures (first in Hindi, rest in English), a cesium-results talk introducing atomic clock fundamentals, and a lithium talk on trapped-atom laser-cooling methodology.
